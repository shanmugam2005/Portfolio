Microservices

Microservices is an architectural style that structures an application as a collection of loosely coupled, independently deployable services. Each microservice is responsible for a specific business functionality and communicates with other services via lightweight protocols, typically over HTTP or messaging queues. This approach allows teams to develop, deploy, and scale services independently, improving flexibility, fault isolation, and overall system scalability. Microservices also support continuous delivery and agile development, enabling rapid iterations and innovation within complex, large-scale systems.
